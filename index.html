WELCOME TO THE HEWITT LAB!

We investigate the mechanisms that guide physiological and regenerative blood formation, focusing on gene regulation, GATA transcription factors, and diseases arising from deregulated transcription factor activity.

What We Study

Research Focus Areas

Our lab bridges molecular biology and bioinformatics expertise to address fundamental questions in hematopoiesis, blood cell differentiation, and stem cell biology.

Transcriptomic Profiling

We use single-cell RNA sequencing and chromatin accessibility profiling to dissect the transcriptional landscape of erythroid progenitors in acute anemia, identifying stress driven cell populations and regulatory networks.

Learn more →

Stress Erythropoiesis Mechanisms

We investigate how environmental exposures, genetic variation, and cell signaling perturbations converge to drive hematological malignancies including acute/chronic anemia and leukemia.

Learn more →

Bioinformatics Frameworks

We develop and apply machine learning models, multi-omics integration pipelines, and bioinformatics workflows to extract actionable biological insights from large, heterogeneous genomic datasets and predict the regulatory mechanisms required for progenitor activity in normal and disease contexts.

Dr. Pooja Roy has successfully defended her PhD thesis!

Dr. Roy's research was focused on understanding the role of Samd14-PI3P axis in regulating erythropoiesis in acute anemia. She is also the recipient of the prestigiuous Thomas H. Publication

New Paper on Samd14's Role in Regulating Autophagy in Anemia

Our latest study identifies that Samd14 acts as a brake on excessive autophagy in anemia stress. These findings reveal a stress-dependent mechanism by which Samd14 sustains progenitor survival and ensures the production of healthy red blood cells during anemia.
