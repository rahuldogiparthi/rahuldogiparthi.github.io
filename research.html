Our Work

Research Projects

We apply cutting-edge genomic and computational approaches to understand how gene regulation is controlled during the differentiation of all the varied cell types that exist in the blood system.

Research Area 01

Cardiac Genomics and Heart Failure

Heart failure remains a leading cause of morbidity and mortality worldwide. Our lab leverages single-nucleus RNA sequencing (snRNA-seq) and ATAC-seq to profile the transcriptional and chromatin accessibility landscape across all major cell types of the human heart.

We are particularly interested in how cardiomyocytes, fibroblasts, endothelial cells, and immune populations respond to pressure overload and ischemic injury, and how intercellular communication networks become dysregulated in the failing myocardium.

snRNA-seq snATAC-seq Cell-cell Signaling Trajectory Analysis Heart Failure Models
Research Area 02

Pulmonary Disease and Vascular Remodeling

Lung diseases such as pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH), pulmonary fibrosis, and COPD involve profound remodeling of the vascular, epithelial, and interstitial compartments. We study these processes using spatial transcriptomics and multi-modal single-cell profiling of human lung biospecimens.

Our work aims to map the cell-type-specific gene expression programs driving vascular lesion formation and fibrotic niche establishment, identifying candidate therapeutic targets in disease-relevant cell states.

Spatial Transcriptomics Visium / Xenium PAH Models IPF Biology Vascular Biology
Research Area 03

Computational Method Development

Biological insight from high-dimensional genomic data requires robust analytical frameworks. Our lab actively develops open-source software for preprocessing, dimensionality reduction, cluster annotation, and regulatory network inference from single-cell and bulk omics data.

We apply and extend methods from machine learning, graph theory, and Bayesian statistics to address specific challenges posed by sparse, noisy, and high-dimensional biological measurements.

Research Area 04

Translational and Clinical Genomics

In close collaboration with clinicians at Nebraska Medicine and national consortium partners, we apply genomic methods directly to patient-derived biospecimens. This translational focus allows us to validate findings from model systems and prioritize mechanistically relevant targets for intervention.

Current collaborative programs include cardiac surgery biobanking, lung transplant genomics, and GWAS-to-function studies linking common genetic variants to cardiovascular risk.
