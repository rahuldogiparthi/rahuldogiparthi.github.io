Experimental Approaches

Tools & Technologies

We employ a broad suite of wet lab assays, genetic models, and functional validation strategies to dissect the molecular regulation of erythropoiesis from the enhancer to the organism level.

Our Approach

From Genome to Function

Understanding how transcriptional regulators and cis-regulatory elements control red blood cell development requires integrating genomic discovery with rigorous experimental validation. Our toolkit spans gene editing, primary cell culture, genomics, imaging, and in vivo mouse genetics.

Functional Validation

Cell-Based Assays

We validate regulatory hypotheses using primary hematopoietic progenitor cells and differentiation systems that faithfully model human and murine erythropoiesis.

🧱

Flow Cytometry

Immunophenotyping & Sorting

We use multiparameter flow cytometry to resolve erythroid differentiation stages using surface markers including CD71, Ter119, CD44, and CD117. This approach allows precise quantification of progenitor populations, assessment of differentiation kinetics, and isolation of defined cell fractions by FACS for downstream genomic or biochemical analysis.

BD LSRFortessa FACSAria
🧪

Colony Forming Unit Assays

CFU-E / BFU-E Quantification

Colony forming unit assays are a cornerstone of our erythroid functional validation pipeline. We culture murine bone marrow or fetal liver progenitors, as well as human CD34+ HSPCs, in semi-solid methylcellulose media to enumerate erythroid progenitor activity under stress and homeostatic conditions, including following genetic perturbation.

MethoCult CFU-E BFU-E
🪘

Lentiviral Transduction

Stable Gene Delivery in HSPCs

We generate lentiviral constructs to stably overexpress or knockdown transcription factors and regulatory genes in primary hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells. This enables gain-of-function and loss-of-function studies in human CD34+ cells and murine LSK progenitors across multi-day erythroid differentiation protocols.

VSV-G pseudotyped CD34+ HSPCs
⚡

RNP Nucleofection

CRISPR-Cas9 in Primary Cells

We deliver pre-assembled ribonucleoprotein (RNP) complexes of Cas9 protein and guide RNA directly into primary hematopoietic progenitors via nucleofection. This approach achieves high on-target editing efficiency with minimal toxicity, enabling precise knockout of transcription factor binding sites, enhancer sequences, and coding exons in human and murine erythroid progenitors.

Lonza 4D Nucleofector Cas9 RNP
🏭

PIP Strips

Phosphoinositide Binding Assays

We use PIP Strip membrane assays to evaluate the phosphoinositide-binding specificity of proteins of interest in the erythroid regulatory network. Purified recombinant protein or cell lysate fractions are incubated with membranes spotted with defined phospholipids, enabling identification of lipid-binding domains relevant to membrane-associated signaling in erythroid development.

Echelon Biosciences Lipid Binding
🔬

Immunofluorescence Microscopy

Protein Localization Studies

We apply immunofluorescence (IF) imaging to visualize the subcellular localization of transcription factors, chromatin regulators, and structural proteins across erythroid differentiation stages. Confocal imaging of bone marrow cytospin preparations and cultured erythroid cells allows us to correlate protein distribution with functional state and genetic background.

Confocal Cytospins
Genomics

Sequencing-Based Approaches

We generate and analyze high-dimensional genomic datasets to map the transcriptional and epigenomic landscape of erythropoiesis at bulk and single-cell resolution.

📥
Bulk RNA-seq
Transcriptome Profiling

We perform poly-A selected and ribo-depleted RNA sequencing on sorted erythroid populations to quantify gene expression changes following genetic perturbation, stress erythropoiesis induction, or cytokine stimulation. Libraries are prepared from as few as 50,000 sorted cells to enable profiling of rare progenitor fractions.

DESeq2 STAR featureCounts
📖
ATAC-seq
Chromatin Accessibility

ATAC-seq is used to map open chromatin regions, nucleosome positioning, and transcription factor footprints across erythroid differentiation. We apply ATAC-seq to compare accessibility landscapes between wild-type and enhancer knockout progenitors, identifying regulatory elements functionally dependent on key erythroid regulators such as GATA-1 and TAL1.

Bowtie2 MACS2 DiffBind
🧲
scRNA-seq by Seq-Well
Single-Cell Transcriptomics

We use the Seq-Well platform for massively parallel single-cell RNA sequencing of primary hematopoietic cells. This portable, low-input method is particularly suited to rare progenitor populations and allows us to map transcriptional heterogeneity, identify novel cell states, and trace differentiation trajectories across erythropoiesis at single-cell resolution in both homeostatic and stress conditions.

Seq-Well S3 Seurat Trajectory
In Vivo Models

Mouse Genetic Models

We leverage precision-engineered mouse models to study the in vivo consequences of disrupting key erythroid regulators and cis-regulatory elements, including under conditions of anemia-induced stress erythropoiesis.

🐀

Samd14 Conditional Knockout

Samd14 CKO • Hematopoietic-specific deletion

The Samd14 conditional knockout model uses Cre-lox technology to achieve hematopoietic-specific deletion of Samd14, a membrane-associated protein we have identified as a regulator of erythroid progenitor survival and stress erythropoiesis. Samd14 CKO mice develop normally under homeostatic conditions but exhibit a significantly impaired recovery response following phlebotomy-induced anemia, revealing a non-redundant role for Samd14 in erythroid regeneration.

Vav1-Cre Stress Erythropoiesis Phlebotomy Model Bone Marrow Analysis
✏️

Samd14 Enhancer Knockout

Enh KO • Cis-regulatory element deletion

The Samd14 enhancer knockout (Enh KO) model uses CRISPR-Cas9-mediated germline deletion to remove a hematopoietic-specific intronic enhancer element that drives Samd14 expression in erythroid progenitors. Enh KO mice display reduced Samd14 expression selectively in hematopoietic tissues, leading to impaired CFU activity and diminished progenitor survival under anemic stress, establishing this enhancer as a critical in vivo regulatory element.

In Vivo Experimental Workflow

Step 01

Model Generation

Breeding, genotyping, and confirmation of CKO or Enh KO alleles by PCR and sequencing.

Step 02

Stress Induction

Phlebotomy-induced acute anemia to interrogate stress erythropoiesis response.

Step 03

Tissue Harvest

Bone marrow, spleen, and peripheral blood collection at defined recovery timepoints.

Step 04

Flow Cytometry

Immunophenotyping of erythroid progenitor stages and quantification of cellularity.

Step 05

Functional Assays

CFU colony assays, blood counts, and reticulocyte quantification.

Step 06

Genomics

RNA-seq and ATAC-seq on sorted populations to map transcriptional changes.

Imaging

Immunofluorescence Studies

Spatial and morphological context complements genomic data. We use immunofluorescence microscopy to visualize protein localization, co-occupancy, and structural changes across erythroid maturation stages.

Cytospin Preparations

Bone marrow cells and cultured erythroid progenitors are prepared as cytospin slides to preserve cell morphology for antibody staining and confocal imaging across differentiation stages.

Transcription Factor Localization

We use validated antibodies against GATA-1, TAL1, KLF1, and Samd14 to map nuclear versus cytoplasmic distribution and assess co-localization between interacting regulatory proteins in erythroid progenitors.

Enucleation and Morphology

Terminal erythroid maturation is assessed by imaging nuclear condensation, enucleation efficiency, and cytoskeletal remodeling using DAPI, Ter119, and F-actin staining across ex vivo differentiation cultures.

Proximity Ligation Assay (PLA)

To detect protein-protein interactions in situ, we perform proximity ligation assays in primary erythroid cells, enabling visualization of endogenous complex formation at single-molecule sensitivity.
